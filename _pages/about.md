<p>I am pursuing my Masters-Thesis at Purdue University, where I am extremely fortunate to be advised by <a href = https://www.cs.purdue.edu/homes/dgleich/>Professor David Gleich</a>. My research interests are in the field of network analysis and machine learning with a current focus on representation learning for higher order connectivity. Among the applications, the problems arising in social networks and mechanism design interest me. I started with a background in Electronics and Telecommunication and have briefly worked on inverse microwave imaging for tumor detection.</p>
