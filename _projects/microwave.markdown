Under the guidance of [Prof. Uday Khankhoje](http://www.ee.iitm.ac.in/~uday/), I worked on inverse microwave scattering for tumor imaging. This technique uses microwave images to reconstruct the permittivity profile of the scatterer, in this case, which is the tumor containing tissue. The equation contains two unknowns: the total field and the contrast. Due to the two unknowns, the problem is formulated into an ill-posed optimization problem. The underlying physics allows one to iteratively solve the equation by updating each unknown alternately while holding the other one constant over a small cross-section of the scatterer for electrically small object. Each such cross-section on the scatterer is equivalent to a pixel wherein either of the unknowns can be safely assumed to be constant. This technique would turn erroneous if the original value of the permittivity was higher than a certain value or if a large area was considered as a pixel. My work here was born out of the tradeoff, posed by large number of pixels, between accuracy and memory requirements.
